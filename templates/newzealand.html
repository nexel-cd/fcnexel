Why Study in NEW-ZEALAND ?

New Zealand is a highly sought after destination for international students due to its unique combination of high-quality education, abundant employment opportunities, and an excellent quality of life. With a practical and modern education system based on the prestigious British system, New Zealand offers a wide range of study options, including state-administered primary and secondary schools, universities, polytechnics, colleges of education, and private training establishments. The polytechnic programs in New Zealand are specifically designed to prepare students for the job market, while 7 of the country's 8 universities consistently rank among the top 500 universities globally.


Additionally, upon completion of their studies, international students are provided with a visa to search for employment opportunities and even have a good chance to migrate to New Zealand. Overall, studying in New Zealand is a truly unique and rewarding experience that provides students with the best of both worlds in terms of education and future prospects.

Career Opportunities in NEW-ZEALAND

New Zealand's key industries, such as computer software development, film, television and commercial production, banking, finance, advertising, property, tourism & hospitality, and food processing, offer a wealth of employment opportunities for international students who choose to study in the country. International students are allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during summer holidays, earning at least the minimum wage of NZ$18.90 per hour.


Graduates from a course in New Zealand that qualifies under the Skilled Migrant Category may apply for a grand job search visa, and their partners may also apply for a work permit during the duration of their study course. Moreover, recent changes in New Zealand's post-study work visa rules have made it even more attractive for international students. For instance, students pursuing level 4 to 6 and non-degree level 7 qualifications are eligible for a two-year open work visa if they study outside Auckland, and students with bachelor and post-graduate level qualifications are eligible for a three-year open work visa. Overall, studying in New Zealand offers unique opportunities and benefits for international students seeking employment and a bright future.

Education Cost in NEW-ZEALAND

New Zealand offers an exceptional study experience with high-quality courses, a high standard of living, and a lower cost compared to other study destinations. The cost of studying in New Zealand varies from city to city, and it is essential to plan accordingly. While tuition fees vary based on the program of study, it is worth noting that New Zealand offers a range of affordable courses across its universities, polytechnics, and private training establishments.


In addition to tuition fees, students should also budget for living expenses, such as accommodation, food, transportation, and healthcare. New Zealand offers a range of accommodation options, including on-campus and off-campus housing, homestays, and shared apartments, to suit different budgets and preferences. With its beautiful landscapes, diverse culture, and friendly people, studying in New Zealand offers a unique experience that is both enriching and affordable.

NEW-ZEALAND Student VISA

Studying in New Zealand is a great opportunity for international students, but it is essential to meet the rules and requirements to obtain a student visa. To apply for a student visa, you must first assess yourself against the policy and ensure that you meet the basic requirements, such as being a genuine student, enrolling in a recognized full-time course, having the financial means to pay for your studies and support yourself, and intending to follow your chosen course and leave the country at the end of your authorized stay. If you are already in New Zealand on a student visa and wish to extend it, you may be able to do so online through certified educational institutes.


It is recommended to apply four weeks before the start of your desired course. You can apply for a visa online or by submitting a paper application. Gathering all necessary documents is crucial for a successful visa application. Once you have submitted your application, you will need to wait for a visa decision, which can take some time. By following the application process carefully, you can ensure a smooth transition to studying in New Zealand.
